## 1. Scope and intent

This section defines the base continuous object of the Coh stack:

1. an admissible state space,
2. a violation or risk functional,
3. a budgeted viability law,
4. a projected dynamics law that enforces admissibility, and
5. an intrinsic time density generated by viability.

The central claim is that **governance is not bolted on afterward**. It is built directly into the geometry of admissible motion. In the strongest form appearing in your source stack, the same machinery also produces the relativistic root-clock and an emergent lapse-like quantity.

## 2. Primitive data

> **Tag**: `[AXIOM]`

Let $\mathcal H$ be a real Hilbert space.

We begin with:

* a state variable $x(t)\in \mathcal H$,
* a violation functional
  \[
  V:\mathcal H\to \mathbb R,
  \]
  assumed proper, lower semicontinuous, and convex on the base core presentation,
* a hard admissibility threshold $\Theta\in\mathbb R$,
* a nonnegative scalar budget $b(t)\in \mathbb R_{\ge 0}$,
* and a drift field $\mathcal F$ on the extended state space.

Define the **safe region**
\[
\mathcal C:=\{x\in\mathcal H:V(x)\le \Theta\}.
\]

Define the **extended state**
\[
z=(x,b)\in \mathcal H\times \mathbb R.
\]

Define the **admissible set**
\[
\mathcal K:=\mathcal C\times \mathbb R_{\ge 0}.
\]

This exact structure appears in the continuous core source.

## 3. Convex admissibility geometry

> **Tag**: `[AXIOM]`

Because $V$ is convex and lower semicontinuous, the sublevel set $\mathcal C=\{V\le \Theta\}$ is closed and convex. Since $\mathbb R_{\ge 0}$ is also closed and convex, the product
\[
\mathcal K=\mathcal C\times \mathbb R_{\ge 0}
\]
is closed and convex.

That matters because all of the clean differential-inclusion machinery depends on it. No convexity, no nice normal cone, no sweeping-process hammer; then the math turns feral.

## 4. Tangent and normal cones

> **Tag**: `[AXIOM]`

For $z\in \mathcal K$, define the Bouligand tangent cone
\[
T_{\mathcal K}(z)
:=
\left\{
v\in \mathcal H\times\mathbb R:
\liminf_{h\downarrow 0}\frac{\operatorname{dist}(z+hv,\mathcal K)}{h}=0
\right\}.
\]

This is the exact tangent-cone definition in the source text.

The proximal/convex normal cone is the polar dual:
\[
N_{\mathcal K}(z)=T_{\mathcal K}(z)^\circ.
\]

For closed convex sets, one also has
\[
\partial I_{\mathcal K}(z)=N_{\mathcal K}(z),
\]
where $I_{\mathcal K}$ is the indicator functional
\[
I_{\mathcal K}(z)=
\begin{cases}
0,&z\in\mathcal K,\\
+\infty,&z\notin\mathcal K.
\end{cases}
\]

This identification is also explicitly stated in the source.

### 4.1 Budget boundary tangent cone

> **Tag**: `[POLICY]`

At a boundary point $(x,0)\in \mathcal K$, the tangent cone in the budget direction is
\[
T_{\mathcal K}(x,0)=\mathcal H\times [0,\infty).
\]

So at $b=0$, an admissible instantaneous velocity must satisfy
\[
\dot b\ge 0.
\]

That is the first half of the boundary law.

## 5. Governed dynamics as a Moreau sweeping process

> **Tag**: `[POLICY]`

The continuous governed system is posed as a projected differential inclusion:
\[
\dot z(t)\in \mathcal F(z(t)) - N_{\mathcal K}(z(t)).
\]

This is the exact core inclusion given in the source.

Interpretation:

* $\mathcal F(z)$ is the desired free drift,
* $N_{\mathcal K}(z)$ is the reaction term that prevents escape from the admissible set,
* subtraction by the normal cone means the system may be "pushed" tangentially inward whenever the free drift points outward.

This is the precise mathematical upgrade from "soft penalty vibes" to "hard viability law."

### 5.1 Existence and forward invariance

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]`

Because $\mathcal K$ is closed and convex and $N_{\mathcal K}$ is maximal monotone, the standard continuation theory for sweeping processes gives a unique absolutely continuous global trajectory
\[
z(t)\in \mathcal K,\qquad t\ge 0,
\]
for admissible initial data. The source states this directly.

So the first foundational theorem is:

### Theorem 5.1 — Global admissible evolution

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]`

Assume the drift $\mathcal F$ is regular enough for the standard sweeping-process well-posedness theory. Then for every $z(0)\in\mathcal K$, there exists a unique absolutely continuous trajectory satisfying
\[
\dot z(t)\in \mathcal F(z(t))-N_{\mathcal K}(z(t)),
\quad z(t)\in\mathcal K\ \forall t\ge 0.
\]

**Meaning:** the system is strictly forward invariant. It never leaves admissibility once started inside it.

## 6. Budget spend law

> **Tag**: `[POLICY]`

Now impose the thermodynamic spend law appearing in the source:
\[
\dot b=-\kappa\left(\frac{dV}{dt}\right)_+,\qquad \kappa>0,
\]
where
\[
a_+:=\max(a,0).
\]

This is the continuous budget law explicitly stated in Volume I.

Interpretation:

* if $V$ is decreasing or flat, no budget is spent,
* if $V$ tries to increase, budget is consumed at rate proportional to the positive variation.

So budget is not a decorative scalar. It is the exact absorber of attempted outward motion in violation space.

## 7. Algebraic boundary-collapse theorem

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]`

This is one of the cleanest results in the whole stack.

At the boundary $b=0$, two facts must hold simultaneously:

1. **Viability from the tangent cone** requires
   \[
   \dot b\ge 0,
   \]
   since $T_{\mathcal K}(x,0)=\mathcal H\times [0,\infty)$.

2. **Physical budget spending** requires
   \[
   \dot b=-\kappa\left(\frac{dV}{dt}\right)_+\le 0.
   \]

The only number that is both $\ge 0$ and $\le 0$ is $0$. Therefore
\[
\dot b=0.
\]

Substituting into the spend law gives
\[
-\kappa\left(\frac{dV}{dt}\right)_+=0
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\left(\frac{dV}{dt}\right)_+=0
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\frac{dV}{dt}\le 0.
\]

That yields:

### Theorem 7.1 — Absorbing budget boundary

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]`

If the system reaches a boundary point with $b=0$, then all risk-increasing motion is algebraically eliminated:
\[
\frac{dV}{dt}\le 0.
\]

Equivalently, outward violation motion cannot occur once the budget is exhausted. This is exactly the theorem stated in the source.

### Why this matters

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]`

This is stronger than a soft penalty. A soft penalty says "bad motion becomes expensive."
This says: at the boundary, **bad motion is not merely discouraged; it becomes kinematically impossible**.

That is the whole dragon-slaying move.

## 8. Chain-rule form of the spend law

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]`

Assume $V$ is differentiable along the trajectory. Then
\[
\frac{dV}{dt}=\langle \nabla V(x(t)),\dot x(t)\rangle.
\]

So the budget equation becomes
\[
\dot b
= -\kappa\big(\langle\nabla V(x),\dot x\rangle\big)_+.
\]

Hence the extended dynamics couple violation geometry and resource depletion through the scalar product of motion with the gradient of violation.

This gives a useful differential inequality for the combined quantity
\[
W(t):=b(t)+\kappa V(x(t)).
\]

Indeed,
\[
\dot W
= \dot b+\kappa\dot V
= -\kappa(\dot V)_+ + \kappa \dot V.
\]

Now split into cases.

If $\dot V\ge 0$, then $(\dot V)_+=\dot V$, hence
\[
\dot W = -\kappa \dot V + \kappa \dot V = 0.
\]

If $\dot V<0$, then $(\dot V)_+=0$, hence
\[
\dot W=\kappa \dot V<0.
\]

Therefore
\[
\dot W\le 0.
\]

So we obtain:

### Proposition 8.1 — Monotone budget-plus-violation functional

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]`

Along any sufficiently regular governed trajectory,
\[
\frac{d}{dt}\big(b+\kappa V\big)\le 0.
\]

This is not spelled out line-by-line in the source, but it follows directly from the stated spend law. The theorem is a legitimate derivation from the provided equations.

This is useful because it gives a crude Lyapunov-style scalar:
\[
b(t)+\kappa V(x(t)) \le b(0)+\kappa V(x(0)).
\]

## 9. Schur-coercive governance coupling

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]` / `[LEMMA-NEEDED]`

The continuous core in the source does not stop at viability cones. It also gives an operator-theoretic template for how a dissipative governance sector can force damping into a conservative physical sector.

Let
\[
z=(u,y)\in \mathcal H_u\oplus \mathcal H_y
\]
and define the coupled linear generator
\[
L=
\begin{pmatrix}
A & B\\
-B^\top & -D
\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\dot z=Lz.
\]

The source assumes:

* $A^\top=-A$, so the physical core is skew-adjoint,
* $D=D^\top\succeq \lambda_{\max}^{-1}I$, so the governance block is strictly dissipative,
* $B$ is the coupling.

Let $S=\ker(A)$ be the neutral drift sector.

Assume the observability condition
\[
|B^\top u|\ge \beta|u|,\qquad u\in S.
\]

Then the effective reduced dynamics on $S$ are governed by
\[
S_{\mathrm{eff}}=BD^{-1}B^\top.
\]

Now compute:
\[
\langle u,S_{\mathrm{eff}}u\rangle
= \langle u,BD^{-1}B^\top u\rangle
= \langle D^{-1/2}B^\top u, D^{-1/2}B^\top u\rangle.
\]

Since $D\succeq \lambda_{\max}^{-1}I$, one has
\[
D^{-1}\succeq \lambda_{\max} I.
\]

Using the source's exact coercive estimate:
\[
\langle u,S_{\mathrm{eff}}u\rangle
\ge
\frac{1}{\lambda_{\max}}|B^\top u|^2
\ge
\frac{\beta^2}{\lambda_{\max}}|u|^2.
\]

This is explicitly given in the archive.

Hence the neutral modes become damped.

From
\[
\frac12\frac{d}{dt}|u|^2 \le -\frac{\beta^2}{\lambda_{\max}}|u|^2,
\]
Grönwall yields
\[
|u(t)|\le e^{-(\beta^2/\lambda_{\max})t}|u(0)|.
\]

This corollary is also explicitly present.

### Theorem 9.1 — Governance-induced damping of neutral drift

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]`

Neutral modes observable through the governance coupling decay exponentially, even though the physical sector generator $A$ is itself conservative.

This is structurally important: the system can remain physically conservative in its bare core while still acquiring enforced damping through lawful coupling.

## 10. Root-clock from viability

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]`

Now we turn to the relativity bridge portion, which appears in both Volume I and Volume IV. The idea is that intrinsic system time is not taken as primitive; it is generated by viability density.

### 10.1 Velocity admissibility barrier

> **Tag**: `[AXIOM]`

Impose a hard velocity cap
\[
|v|<c.
\]

Define the half-log barrier
\[
\widetilde V(v):=-\frac12\log\left(1-\frac{|v|^2}{c^2}\right).
\]

This is the barrier whose viability density generates the root-clock. The source states the evaluated exponential form directly.

Now compute:
\[
e^{-\widetilde V(v)}
= \exp\left(\frac12\log\left(1-\frac{|v|^2}{c^2}\right)\right)
= \sqrt{1-\frac{|v|^2}{c^2}}.
\]

Define intrinsic time density by
\[
\frac{d\tau}{dt}:=e^{-\widetilde V(v)}
= \sqrt{1-\frac{|v|^2}{c^2}}.
\]

Then, since $dx=v\,dt$,
\[
d\tau^2
= \left(1-\frac{|v|^2}{c^2}\right)dt^2
= dt^2-\frac{|dx|^2}{c^2}.
\]

So:

### Theorem 10.1 — Minkowski interval from the half-log barrier

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]`

The viability-induced root-clock satisfies
\[
d\tau=\sqrt{1-\frac{|v|^2}{c^2}}\,dt
\]
and therefore
\[
d\tau^2=dt^2-\frac{dx^2}{c^2}.
\]

This is explicitly claimed in the source material.

### Interpretation

In this presentation, the Minkowski interval is not postulated first and then philosophically admired afterward. It emerges from the barrier geometry of admissible motion.

That is a bold claim. But within your internal canon, this is the intended constitutive derivation.

## 13. Hessian metric on velocity space

> **Tag**: `[PROVED]` / `[LEMMA-NEEDED]`

Volume IV also gives a geometric refinement: the barrier induces a Hessian metric on the admissible velocity ball.

Define
\[
\widetilde V(v)=-\frac12\log\left(1-\frac{|v|^2}{c^2}\right).
\]

Then the Hessian metric is
\[
g_{ij}(v)=\frac{\partial^2\widetilde V}{\partial v_i\partial v_j}.
\]

Let $s=|v|^2/c^2$. Then
\[
\widetilde V(v)=-\frac12\log(1-s).
\]

First derivative:
\[
\frac{\partial \widetilde V}{\partial v_i}
= \frac{v_i}{c^2(1-s)}.
\]

Second derivative:
\[
g_{ij}(v)
= \frac{\delta_{ij}}{c^2(1-|v|^2/c^2)}
+ \frac{2v_i v_j}{c^4(1-|v|^2/c^2)^2}.
\]

So the barrier metric blows up as $|v|\to c^{-}$, which matches the idea that the boundary is infinitely far in the induced geometry.

# Section I — Continuous Core and Geometric Governance

Let $\mathcal H$ be a real Hilbert space and let $V:\mathcal H\to\mathbb R$ be a proper lower semicontinuous convex violation functional. Fix a hard admissibility threshold $\Theta\in\mathbb R$ and define the safe region
\[
\mathcal C=\{x\in\mathcal H:V(x)\le \Theta\}.
\]

Let $b\in\mathbb R_{\ge 0}$ be the thermodynamic budget and define the extended state
\[
z=(x,b)\in \mathcal H\times \mathbb R.
\]

The admissible domain is
\[
\mathcal K=\mathcal C\times \mathbb R_{\ge 0}.
\]

For $z\in\mathcal K$, the Bouligand tangent cone is
\[
T_{\mathcal K}(z)=
\left\{
v:
\liminf_{h\downarrow 0}\frac{\operatorname{dist}(z+hv,\mathcal K)}{h}=0
\right\},
\]
and the normal cone is its polar dual
\[
N_{\mathcal K}(z)=T_{\mathcal K}(z)^\circ=\partial I_{\mathcal K}(z).
\]

The governed continuous dynamics are defined by the Moreau-type projected inclusion
\[
\dot z(t)\in \mathcal F(z(t)) - N_{\mathcal K}(z(t)).
\]
Since $\mathcal K$ is closed and convex, this law yields a forward-invariant admissible trajectory under the standard well-posedness hypotheses.

The budget obeys the positive-variation spend law
\[
\dot b = -\kappa \left(\frac{dV}{dt}\right)_+,\qquad \kappa>0.
\]

At the budget boundary $b=0$, admissibility requires
\[
T_{\mathcal K}(x,0)=\mathcal H\times [0,\infty),
\]
hence $\dot b\ge 0$, while the spend law gives $\dot b\le 0$. Therefore $\dot b=0$, so
\[
\left(\frac{dV}{dt}\right)_+=0,
\qquad
\frac{dV}{dt}\le 0.
\]

Thus all risk-increasing motion is eliminated at the exhausted-budget boundary.

Moreover, the combined quantity
\[
W(t)=b(t)+\kappa V(x(t))
\]
is monotone nonincreasing, because
\[
\dot W = -\kappa(\dot V)_+ + \kappa \dot V \le 0.
\]

To generate intrinsic system time, define the lawfulness power
\[
\Lambda(x)=b(x)+\alpha \mathcal D_a(x)\ge 0,
\]
where $\mathcal D_a\ge 0$ is the admissible physical dissipation rate. The intrinsic root-clock is
\[
\frac{d\tau}{dt}=\sqrt{\Lambda(x(t))}.
\]

Hence the horizon set
\[
\partial \mathcal M_H=\{x:\Lambda(x)=0\}
=\{b=0\}\cap\{\mathcal D_a=0\}
\]
is a strict freezing set:
\[
\Lambda=0 \implies \frac{d\tau}{dt}=0.
\]

In the velocity-barrier specialization, impose $|v|<c$ and define
\[
\widetilde V(v)=-\frac12\log\left(1-\frac{|v|^2}{c^2}\right).
\]

Then the viability density is
\[
e^{-\widetilde V(v)}=\sqrt{1-\frac{|v|^2}{c^2}},
\]
so the root-clock becomes
\[
d\tau=\sqrt{1-\frac{|v|^2}{c^2}}\,dt,
\]
and therefore
\[
d\tau^2=dt^2-\frac{dx^2}{c^2}.
\]

Thus the Minkowski proper-time interval appears as the algebraic expression of the half-log admissibility barrier.

If the system is written in ADM form with vanishing shift,
\[
ds^2=-N^2dt^2+\gamma_{ij}dx^idx^j,
\]
then for stationary observers $d\tau=Ndt$, so one identifies
\[
N=\sqrt{\Lambda}=\sqrt{b+\alpha\mathcal D_a}.
\]

This yields the constitutive lapse bridge from governance to effective spacetime timing.
